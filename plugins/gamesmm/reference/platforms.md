# Platform Norms — working cheat sheet for adapting a post

Voice doesn't change with the platform; length, format, link placement, and hashtags do. Below is what to do for each. Rationale and sources are in [research-notes.md](research-notes.md). If a platform isn't in the user's profile, don't write for it.

| Platform | Limit | Visible before "more" | Optimum | Links | Hashtags |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| X / Twitter | 280 (Premium 25,000) | fully visible | 71–100 chars | **in reply, not the post** | 0–2 |
| Threads | 500 (attachment up to 10,000) | ~first lines | short, thread if long | up to 5, first = preview | **1 topic tag** |
| Reddit | depends on the sub | title decides | to the point, no filler | often banned in promo posts | none |
| Telegram | 4096 | ~first lines in feed | 1 idea per post | freely | keywords, not decoration |
| Instagram | 2200 | ~125 characters | caption <30 words | bio only | **max 5** |
| YouTube | title ~70, description large | description until "Show more" | title specific | Steam link in description line 1 | 3–5 in description |
| TikTok | caption up to 2200 | ~100 characters | hook in the video, not the text | in bio | on-topic, #FYP is useless |

## Platform habits (more important than limits)

**X / Twitter.** The algorithm throttles outbound links — put the link in the first reply, not the main tweet. Short wins. For indies, X is a channel to press and streamers, not to buyers; don't expect sales from followers.

**Threads.** Replies account for almost half of all views: a post that invites a reply outperforms a monologue. A topic tag boosts reach (1 per post, up to 50 characters). There's no link penalty like on X — don't carry that caution over blindly. Post 2–5 times/week.

**Reddit.** "Show, don't sell" — a post that's just a link, or a wishlist CTA in the title, gets you banned. Norm: ~9 helpful actions (comments, replies) per 1 promo post. r/gamedev isn't for showcasing (they'll point you to r/indiegames), links only when contextual (post-mortem, analytics). r/indiegames — promo ≤2×/week, gameplay media required, no store links. Posting the same thing to several subs within an hour → ban. Title can't be edited after posting.

**Telegram.** Keywords at the start of the post — Public Post Search across channels now runs on this. Reactions, polls, comments (via a discussion group) — turn them on, they drive reach. HTML formatting is supported. Genuine comments on niche channels is a growth tactic that works.

**Instagram.** Key point in the first ~125 characters (then it truncates to "more"). Max 5 hashtags — more than that and the post won't publish. Reach from IG has historically been weak for indie games — low priority, short caption + strong media.

**YouTube.** Two surfaces: Shorts (discovery — hook in 1–2 sec, Steam link in the first line of the description + a spoken CTA "link in the description") and long-form devlogs (cold open, the first 30 sec are critical). Title should be specific, not "Devlog #14": "I rebuilt my combat system from scratch" beats it on CTR by a wide margin. North star is wishlists, not views.

**TikTok (starting from zero).** Follower count and past hits do NOT affect landing on the FYP — every video ranks on its own by watch time (completion rate). Posting frequency doesn't matter either — feel free to experiment. Hook in the first 3 seconds. Captions are mandatory (30% watch with sound off). Stay in a narrow niche, not "about everything." #FYP gives no boost. Send viewers to Steam/Discord — "don't build your castle on someone else's land."

## Indie gamedev: the frame over every platform

Social media is a funnel to Steam wishlists, not a direct sales channel. Without a demo, posts barely move visibility (the threshold is ~7,000 wishlists for Popular Upcoming). Reddit and TikTok produce wishlist spikes, X is your route to press/streamers, Discord is for superfans (one server per studio, not per game).

## Content mix (if the user posts regularly, not just a one-off)

3–5 recurring themes, with promo as the minority: roughly 70% value/personal, 20% others'/community, 10% direct promo. Working formats for a devlog: Screenshot Saturday, before/after GIFs, weekly recaps, an honest account of a failure and how it got fixed.
